# Revelation Progression Rules

## Core Rule

Every thread should move from discovery to consequence to changed conditions. Repetition is allowed only when the repetition itself changes meaning or cost.

## Thread Shape

- Depth 0: introduce the operational problem.
- Depth 1: show consequence or complication from a chosen response.
- Depth 2: force a character, trust, contamination, availability, field-support, or command tradeoff, or move the threat into base/interlude life.
- Depth 3+: only continue if the thread materially changes state, unlocks a mission, breaks containment, or resolves at cost.

## Short Branches

Short branches are acceptable. They should:

- resolve in one follow-up or interlude,
- write a concrete cost or benefit,
- not pretend to be a major mystery.

## Long Branches

Long branches must earn their length. They should:

- affect at least two domains, such as mission plus base, or a character plus command/field support,
- include at least one interlude beat,
- change character state,
- create a later triggered event,
- have a visible resolution, mutation, or serious loss.

## Follow-Up Quality Tests

A follow-up fails if it only says:

- the signal repeated,
- the room felt stranger,
- the artifact hummed again,
- someone heard the same voice again,
- records contradicted themselves without consequence.

A follow-up passes if it does at least one:

- changes availability, individual morale, mental state, trust, contamination, field support, command pressure, or site state,
- forces a player concession,
- makes an officer/squad member act differently,
- moves the anomaly into a new context,
- closes a question and opens a more specific one,
- creates a later mission or interlude trigger.

## Interlude Placement

Every mission should queue or qualify for at least one interlude. Interludes should show what the mission did to people and systems.

- Clean success: decompression with subtle residue.
- Partial success: friction, fatigue, individual morale damage, field-support cost, contested report.
- Failure: blame, quarantine, refusal, casualty handling, command pressure.
- Artifact recovery: lab window, clinic, command corridor, or logistics beat.

## Branch Outcome Differentiation

Choices should not be cosmetic. Branches should differ in:

- immediate text,
- state changes,
- risk domain,
- follow-up target,
- character affected,
- individual or field-support pressure,
- later trigger.

## Character-Owned Plans

Room-entry choices should usually be plans proposed by specific characters.

- Brooks proposes command/personnel handling.
- Owen proposes containment and procedure.
- Iyad proposes measurement and language controls.
- Ross proposes direct tactical action.
- Park proposes evidence-system handling.
- Torah proposes or enables resonance contact, usually at contamination risk.

If a character is unavailable, unfit, badly injured, heavily contaminated, or mentally blocked, their plan should disappear from the available choices. If they are strained but still available, the plan remains but its success odds fall.

Plan outcomes should branch through chance:

- strong success: better than intended, lower cost, or cleaner evidence,
- success: intended outcome with normal cost,
- partial: useful progress with visible complication,
- failure: plan does not work and pressures the character,
- catastrophe: plan backfires and damages the proposing character or another named person.

## End States

Threads should eventually reach one of:

- `resolved_clean`
- `resolved_at_cost`
- `contained_unstable`
- `mutated_into_new_thread`
- `escaped_containment`
- `absorbed_by_larger_arc`

Do not leave all threads as permanent ambiguous residue.
