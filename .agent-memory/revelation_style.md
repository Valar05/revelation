# Revelation Narrative Tone & Writing Style

## Core Voice

Revelation uses restrained symbolic horror. The narration is grounded, procedural, observant, emotionally controlled, and quietly escalating. The world becomes stranger while the prose remains disciplined. That contrast is critical.

The style draws from SCP field reports, military procedural fiction, restrained horror, theological symbolism, modern cosmic horror, and grounded tactical fiction. It must not read like wiki entries alone. Human texture matters: meals, smoke breaks, awkward conversation, fatigue, and ordinary operational life make the supernatural believable.

## Prose Rules

Use short-to-medium sentences, concrete sensory detail, restrained emotional language, clean readability, and deliberate pacing. Avoid ornate prose, adjective stacking, melodrama, exposition dumps, edgy nihilism, pseudo-Lovecraft diction, and fantasy terminology.

Anomalies are described observationally first, emotionally second, interpretively last. Do not immediately call things evil, demonic, cosmic, or incomprehensible.

Preferred sensory language: heat, vibration, pressure, static, resonance, breath, smoke, humidity, mechanical noise, fluorescent lighting, industrial texture, skin sensation, and silence.

## Characters

Characters behave like trained personnel, scientists, soldiers, exhausted experts, and professionals under strain. Fear appears through hesitation, silence, ritualization, fixation, operational breakdown, altered behavior, and understatement.

Torah speaks simply, directly, and calmly. He avoids speeches, sarcasm, and intellectual display. His weight appears through silence, action, endurance, and brief observations.

Brooks uses dry humor, practical language, and blunt phrasing. She cuts tension, protects people indirectly, and behaves like an experienced squad leader carrying accumulated stress.

Scientists are sleep deprived, obsessed, intelligent, procedural, and unsettled. They are not theatrical or cartoonishly mad. Their fear appears when data stops making sense.

## Dialogue

Dialogue is natural, understated, dry, professional, and situational. Characters rarely monologue or fully explain themselves. Humor can exist, but only as pressure relief, character texture, or squad shorthand. Avoid internet irony, meme humor, genre parody, and drama-undercutting quips.

## Formats

Preferred forms: SITREPs, transcripts, after-action reports, mission narration, containment notes, field recordings, artifact reports, debriefs, and Institute documentation.

SITREPs should be concise, procedural, informative, and tense.

## Mystery

The setting never fully explains itself. Theories conflict. Interpretations differ. Measurements contradict. Faith and science overlap imperfectly. The audience should feel growing understanding and growing uncertainty at the same time.

## Forbidden Tendencies

Avoid Marvel-style banter, excessive profanity, melodrama, exposition dumps, edgy suffering, anime power escalation language, generic cult chanting, casual irreverence, and frequent use of words like eldritch, unspeakable, madness, ancient evil, and incomprehensible. Specificity is stronger than cliche.
