# Revelation Foundational Systems

Revelation is an AI-generated narrated tactical horror roguelike built around symbolic anomalies, squad missions, procedural narrative events, persistent artifacts, and escalating metaphysical contamination.

The player controls Torah, his assigned squad, mission decisions, tactical responses, artifact handling, and containment outcomes. The player does not directly control action combat. Play unfolds through SITREPs, mission choices, squad responses, anomaly interactions, procedural consequences, hidden systems, and escalating symbolic effects.

## Core Premise

Humanity is experiencing anomalous symbolic events. These events violate known physics, react to language and belief, generate measurable resonance, alter matter and consciousness, create artifacts and entities, and escalate over time.

The Institute investigates, contains, recovers, protects civilians, and studies symbolic resonance. Torah is one of its first successful symbolic assets: a human capable of interacting directly with high-level anomalies without immediate death or collapse.

## Core Loop

1. Receive SITREP.
2. Review anomaly data.
3. Assemble squad and equipment.
4. Deploy to mission.
5. Resolve procedural narrative encounters.
6. Suffer consequences.
7. Recover artifacts, injuries, contamination, or knowledge.
8. Return to base.
9. Debrief, research, treat, upgrade, reassign, or contain.
10. Deploy again.

## Systems

- Squad members have hidden stats, fears, symbolic affinity, stress thresholds, injuries, contamination, relationships, and combat capability.
- Stress accumulates through combat, anomaly exposure, civilian casualties, theological contradiction, isolation, failed actions, and squad loss.
- Symbolic resonance turns language, names, myths, geometry, rituals, and emotional meaning into measurable hazards.
- Artifacts are persistent, useful, contaminating, poorly understood, and capable of changing future missions.
- Equipment is grounded and field-usable: armor, meters, restraints, dampeners, seals, arrays, scanners, and industrial containment gear.
- Failure creates future narrative states rather than simply ending the run.

## Long-Term Escalation

Later content may include symbolic outbreaks, impossible architecture, global resonance events, mythological convergence, language-reactive environments, theological warfare, and reality restructuring. Answers should remain partial. Understanding and uncertainty should grow together.
