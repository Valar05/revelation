# Revelation Project-Authored Anomaly Exemplars Plan

## Purpose

The licensed corpus can provide scripture, folklore, procedure, quarantine, stress, and tactical texture, but it cannot replace authored Revelation examples. The SCP-shaped gap should be filled with original project-authored anomaly exemplars that teach the generator structure, pacing, voice, and follow-through without importing CC BY-SA story material.

## Target Corpus

Create 24 to 36 original exemplars before relying on bulk generation.

- 8 low-risk field incidents: contained anomalies, ambiguous readings, minor personnel strain.
- 8 medium-risk missions: casualties, quarantine, squad refusal, artifact recovery, partial containment.
- 6 high-risk escalations: symbolic spread, command breakdown, civilian harm, mutiny/concession pressure.
- 4 base-layer incidents: lab containment, debriefing failure, artifact storage, staff politics.
- 4 character arc exemplars: Torah, Brooks, one scientist, one recurring squad member.
- 8 interlude glue exemplars: debrief, smoke break, meal, clinic, barracks, lab window, logistics, command corridor.
- Optional 4 late-game exemplars: architecture shifts, mass resonance, contradictory theology, reality rewriting.

## Required Shape Per Exemplar

Each exemplar should include:

- SITREP
- objectives
- initial encounter
- 3 to 5 player choices
- probabilistic officer/squad resolution notes
- at least one failed-choice branch
- at least one follow-up hook
- at least one second follow-up that changes the state
- character morale, mental state, trust, contamination, availability, or field-support consequences
- at least one between-mission interlude hook
- corpus anchors used
- writing notes for tone and forbidden moves

## Follow-Up Rules

Follow-ups must progress rather than echo.

- Depth 0: discovery or operational problem.
- Depth 1: consequence, complication, or decision cost.
- Depth 2: irreversible change, new risk, person-specific arc, interlude consequence, or base-layer aftershock.
- Triggered follow-ups: tied to concrete state such as contamination, stress, refusal, individual morale, mental state, trust damage, artifact possession, civilian death, field-support shortage, or Torah resonance use.

## Style Requirements

- Keep prose readable and restrained.
- Use operational language without becoming a wiki entry.
- Let fear appear through hesitation, procedure, fatigue, refusal, silence, and small errors.
- Use corpus material as circumstances and symbolic structures, not as long quotation.
- Avoid SCP terminology, SCP formatting mimicry, and copied anomaly concepts.

## Production Workflow

1. Draft 3 seed exemplars manually with strict voice control.
2. Generate 6 variants from those seeds using corpus anchors.
3. Audit each for follow-up progression, readable prose, and actual state changes.
4. Promote passing examples into `generated/corpus/project_authored_exemplars/`.
5. Index promoted examples as a first-class corpus category.
6. Add interlude exemplars from `revelation_interlude_glue_layer.md` before scaling mission generation.
7. Use those exemplars as the highest-priority retrieval source for new room and interlude generation.

## Acceptance Criteria

The fourth lane is ready when a generated mission can:

- name its corpus anchors,
- explain its follow-up chain,
- produce at least one branch that escalates materially,
- attach consequences to individual morale, mental state, squad stress, contamination, trust, availability, or field support,
- queue or resolve a debrief, smoke break, meal, clinic, barracks, lab, logistics, or command-corridor beat,
- preserve Revelation tone without SCP license exposure.
