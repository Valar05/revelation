# Revelation Corpus Strategy

Room ideas should come from a license-compatible corpus and be transformed into original Revelation missions. The corpus supplies symbolic structures, procedures, contradictions, artifacts, and escalation patterns. It does not supply copied prose, copied characters, or direct doctrinal answers.

## Source Classes

- Public-domain Bible translations and commentarial references where license permits use.
- Public-domain Torah/Hebrew Bible translations and references where license permits use.
- Public-domain or explicitly licensed Quran translations. Translation copyright must be tracked.
- SCP Wiki material under CC BY-SA 3.0, with article-level attribution and share-alike obligations. Do not use SCP-173's former image or other non-compatible media.

## Required Corpus Metadata

Every indexed source should store title, source URL or local origin, translator/author where known, license, attribution requirement, compatibility notes, and whether the source can be redistributed in generated project files.

Every generated mission should list corpus anchors such as `corpus_artifact_ids`, source title, source moment, transformed procedure, room application, and follow-up application.

## Revelation Needs

Index for these needs first:

- name_reactivity
- forbidden_or_effective_speech
- ritual_procedure
- covenant_or_command
- plague_or_outbreak
- prophetic_dream_or_vision
- angelic_or_messenger_encounter
- judgment_or_seal
- artifact_relic_text
- purity_contamination_boundary
- sacrifice_or_substitution
- wilderness_trial
- impossible_architecture
- institutional_containment
- anomalous_object_protocol
- memetic_or_symbolic_hazard
- squad_stress_and_failure

## Transformation Rule

Transform source circumstance, procedure, evidence, escalation, and consequence into Institute missions. Do not copy sacred text passages into casual flavor. Do not flatten theological material into generic magic. Keep ambiguity: faith and science both produce partial models.
